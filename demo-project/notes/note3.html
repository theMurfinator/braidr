Character Arc: Aragorn

The reluctant king who must overcome his ancestor's failure.

Core Wound

Isildur — his direct ancestor — had the chance to destroy the One Ring and failed. Aragorn carries that shame. He doubts whether the weakness is in his blood.

Arc Structure

  • Act 1: Hiding — lives as "Strider," a ranger. Refuses his birthright.

  • Act 2: Testing — forced into leadership after Gandalf falls. Boromir's death forces him to accept responsibility.

  • Act 3: Claiming — takes the Paths of the Dead. Only the true king can command the oathbreakers. He chooses to be king.

  • Climax: The coronation — and the moment he bows to the hobbits.

Key Relationships

Arwen: The stakes. If he doesn't become king, she sails to the Undying Lands. Her mortality is the price of their love — and his motivation.

Boromir: The mirror. Boromir is what Aragorn fears becoming — a good man broken by desperation and the Ring's temptation. Boromir's death is Aragorn's catalyst.

Gandalf: The mentor who believes in him before he believes in himself.

Writing Notes

Aragorn should feel most like a king when he's not trying. The healing hands scene, the way he listens to counsel, the instinct to protect. The crown is just the world catching up to who he already is.
