The One Ring

The central MacGuffin and source of all conflict. Created by Sauron in the fires of Mount Doom to control the other Rings of Power.

Properties

  • Invisibility when worn (side effect, not primary purpose)
  • Extends the wearer's life unnaturally
  • Corrupts the will over time — the longer you carry it, the harder it is to give up
  • Responds to Sauron's will — wants to be found
  • Can only be destroyed where it was made: the Crack of Doom

Key Theme

The Ring represents power that corrupts absolutely. Every character who encounters it is tested. The hierarchy of resistance:

  1. Tom Bombadil — immune (outside the system entirely)
  2. Sam — resists easily (no ambition, only love)
  3. Aragorn — refuses it (knows his ancestor's weakness)
  4. Gandalf — refuses it (knows he'd become a tyrant)
  5. Galadriel — refuses it (passes the test)
  6. Faramir — refuses it (quality of mercy)
  7. Frodo — carries it but ultimately fails at the Crack of Doom
  8. Boromir — succumbs to temptation
  9. Gollum — totally enslaved

Writing Notes

The Ring should feel heavier in the prose as the story progresses. Early scenes: Frodo barely notices it. Middle: it's a weight around his neck. Final approach to Mordor: it's crushing him. The physical weight is a metaphor for moral burden.
